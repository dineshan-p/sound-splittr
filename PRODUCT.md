# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo DJs performing live who need to quickly split songs into stems (vocals, drums, bass, melody) for live remixing, mashups, and acapellas. They use Sound Splittr in rehearsal and on-stage contexts where speed, reliability, and clear feedback matter more than everything else. They are technically literate but not researchers — they care about getting the right stem, fast, and knowing it sounds good.

## Product Purpose

Sound Splittr separates audio files into four isolated stems using Demucs AI, giving DJs the raw material for live performance. The product exists to make stem separation as frictionless as possible: upload a song, watch progress, audition stems, download what you need. Success means a DJ can go from "I need the acapella of this track" to having it ready for their set in under five minutes.

## Brand Personality

**Technical, transparent, open.** Sound Splittr respects the user's intelligence. It shows what's happening under the hood — which model is running, how long processing takes, which GPU is active — without being intimidating. It's the feel of a well-built instrument: precise, honest, no-nonsense, but never sterile. Think Ableton's technical clarity mixed with the openness of a well-documented terminal tool.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate SaaS cream**: No generic blue-white startup aesthetic, no "trust the process" copy, no stock-photo energy. This is a music tool built by and for musicians, not a CRM wrapped in gradients.
- **Minimal to the point of cold**: Don't strip so much personality it feels sterile. Music tools should feel alive — there should be warmth in the details, personality in the microcopy, and a sense that someone who makes music built this.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work.** Progress, model info, GPU status, and processing stages are visible and honest. The user always knows what's happening and why.
2. **Speed first, polish second.** The interface should feel instant — responses, transitions, feedback. Performance is a feature.
3. **Musical, not corporate.** Visual language draws from music production tools and audio interfaces, not SaaS dashboards. Dark themes feel natural; warmth comes through color choices and micro-interactions, not corporate blue.
4. **Transparent by default.** Settings are visible inline, not hidden behind "advanced" toggles. Model choices, output formats, and quality settings are explained when they matter.
5. **Built for focus.** DJs need to work quickly without distraction. The interface surfaces what's needed next and keeps everything else out of the way.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. Color choices must maintain sufficient contrast ratios. Interactive elements should be keyboard-accessible. Color should never be the sole indicator of state — always pair with icons, labels, or text.
